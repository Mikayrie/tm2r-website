Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

COPD is a chronic, progressive lung disease that obstructs airflow and causes persistent breathing problems. It is the third leading cause of death worldwide but is largely preventable and treatable.

Symptoms

The symptoms of COPD often develop slowly and worsen over time. They may be mild at first, but become more severe as the disease progresses.

  • A persistent or chronic cough, often called "smoker's cough," that may produce mucus (sputum).
  • Shortness of breath, especially during physical activity.
  • Wheezing or a whistling sound when you breathe.
  • Chest tightness.
  • Frequent respiratory infections, such as the flu or pneumonia.

Causes and Risk Factors

COPD is a result of long-term exposure to harmful irritants that damage the lungs and airways. The primary causes and risk factors include:

  • Tobacco Smoking: This is the most common cause of COPD, accounting for over 70% of cases in high-income countries. This includes smoking cigarettes, cigars, pipes, and e-cigarettes.
  • Air Pollution: Long-term exposure to indoor air pollution (e.g., from solid fuels used for cooking and heating) and outdoor air pollution are significant risk factors, particularly in low- and middle-income countries.
  • Occupational Exposures: Exposure to dust, chemical fumes, and other irritants in the workplace can contribute to the development of COPD.
  • Genetics: A rare genetic condition called alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency (AATD) is a less common cause of COPD.

Prevention and Management

While there is no cure for COPD, its progression can be significantly slowed and symptoms managed. The most important preventive measure is:

  • Quitting Smoking: This is the most effective way to prevent the disease from getting worse.
  • Avoiding Lung Irritants: Minimize exposure to secondhand smoke and air pollution.
  • Getting Vaccinated: Stay up to date on vaccines for respiratory infections like the flu and pneumonia to prevent dangerous flare-ups.
  • Pulmonary Rehabilitation: This program can help improve breathing, increase physical activity, and enhance your quality of life.
