Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infection of the lungs that can cause mild to severe illness in people of all ages. It is the single largest infectious cause of death in children worldwide. The lungs are made up of small sacs called alveoli, which fill with air when a healthy person breathes. When an individual has pneumonia, the alveoli are filled with pus and fluid, which makes breathing painful and limits oxygen intake.

Symptoms

The signs and symptoms of pneumonia vary from mild to severe, depending on factors such as the type of germ causing the infection, and your age and overall health. Mild signs and symptoms are often similar to those of a cold or flu, but they last longer.

  • Chest pain when you breathe or cough.
  • Cough which may produce phlegm.
  • Fatigue, fever, sweating and shaking chills.
  • Shortness of breath.

Causes and Risk Factors

Pneumonia can be caused by a number of infectious agents, including viruses, bacteria and fungi. *Streptococcus pneumoniae* is the most common cause of bacterial pneumonia in children. The pathogens can be spread through airborne droplets from a cough or sneeze (World Health Organization, 2022).

  • Bacteria and Viruses: Common causes that are present in the air we breathe.
  • Weakened Immune System: People with compromised immune systems (due to HIV/AIDS, chemotherapy, or organ transplant) are at higher risk.
  • Pre-existing Lung Disease: Conditions like COPD or asthma increase risk.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages the lungs' natural defenses against respiratory infections.

Prevention and Management

Pneumonia is preventable and treatable. Prevention includes immunization, adequate nutrition, and addressing environmental factors. Treatment for pneumonia depends on the type and severity.

  • Vaccination: Immunization against Hib, pneumococcus, measles and whooping cough is the most effective way to prevent pneumonia.

  • Treatment: Bacterial pneumonia can be treated with antibiotics. Hospitalization may be needed for severe cases.
