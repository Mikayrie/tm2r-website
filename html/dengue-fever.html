Dengue Fever

Dengue is a viral infection transmitted to humans through the bite of infected mosquitoes. While many infections are asymptomatic or produce only mild illness, dengue can occasionally cause more severe cases and even death.

Symptoms

Most people with dengue have mild or no symptoms and will get better in 1–2 weeks. When symptoms do occur, they usually begin 4–10 days after infection and include:

  • High fever (40°C/104°F)
  • Severe headache and pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pains
  • Nausea, vomiting, and a rash

Warning signs of severe dengue, which require immediate medical attention, include severe abdominal pain and persistent vomiting.

Causes and Risk Factors

Dengue is caused by the dengue virus (DENV), which is transmitted by female mosquitoes, mainly of the species Aedes aegypti. The virus has four serotypes, so it is possible to be infected four times. The mosquitoes are most active during the day.

Prevention and Management

There is no specific treatment for dengue fever. The focus is on treating pain symptoms. Most cases can be treated at home with pain medicine. Preventing mosquito bites is the most effective form of prevention.

  • Use insect repellent, wear long-sleeved shirts and long pants.
  • Control mosquitoes inside and outside your home.
  • Prevent mosquito breeding in and around your home by removing standing water.
