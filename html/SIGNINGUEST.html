Infectious Diseases

Infectious diseases are caused by organisms like bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. They can spread from person to person, from animals, or from the environment. Explore this section to learn more.

Bird Flu

Symptoms: Fever, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, severe respiratory illness.

Prevention: Avoid contact with sick birds, cook poultry thoroughly, practice good hygiene.

Chickenpox

Symptoms: Itchy rash with fluid-filled blisters, fever, tiredness, headache.

Prevention: The most effective prevention is the chickenpox (varicella) vaccine.

Cholera

Symptoms: Severe watery diarrhea, vomiting, muscle cramps, rapid dehydration.

Prevention: Drink and use safe water, wash hands often, cook food well.

Dengue Fever

Symptoms: High fever, severe headache, pain behind eyes, muscle/joint pain, rash.

Prevention: Eliminate mosquito breeding sites, use repellents, wear protective clothing.

Diarrhea

Symptoms: Loose, watery stools three or more times a day, abdominal cramps.

Prevention: Access to safe drinking-water, good personal and food hygiene.

Hepatitis

Symptoms: Fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain, and jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes).

Prevention: Vaccinations (for Hep A/B), practicing safe sex, avoiding shared needles.

HIV

Symptoms: Early flu-like symptoms, can be asymptomatic for years before progressing.

Prevention: Safe sex practices, not sharing needles, and preventive medicines like PrEP.

Influenza (Flu)

Symptoms: Fever, cough, sore throat, runny nose, body aches, and fatigue.

Prevention: Annual flu vaccination, frequent hand washing, avoid touching face.

Leptospirosis

Symptoms: High fever, headache, chills, muscle aches, vomiting, red eyes.

Prevention: Avoid swimming or wading in potentially contaminated water or soil.

Malaria

Symptoms: Fever, chills, and flu-like illness. Can be severe and life-threatening.

Prevention: Use of mosquito nets, insect repellent, and preventive medication.

Measles

Symptoms: High fever, cough, runny nose, watery eyes, followed by a widespread rash.

Prevention: The MMR (measles, mumps, and rubella) vaccine is highly effective.

Pneumonia

Symptoms: Cough with phlegm, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing.

Prevention: Vaccinations (pneumococcal, flu), hand washing, not smoking.

Rabies

Symptoms: Fever, headache, progressing to confusion, agitation, and paralysis.

Prevention: Vaccinate pets, avoid contact with wild animals, seek immediate care after a bite.

Tuberculosis (TB)

Symptoms: Chronic cough, weight loss, night sweats, fever.

Prevention: BCG vaccination, early screening, well-ventilated areas.

Lifestyle Diseases

This section covers non-communicable diseases (NCDs), often linked to factors like diet, physical activity, and environment. Learn about their symptoms and how proactive choices can help in prevention and management.

Arthritis

Symptoms: Joint pain, stiffness, swelling, decreased range of motion.

Prevention: Maintain a healthy weight, control blood sugar, exercise, and avoid injuries.

Asthma

Symptoms: Shortness of breath, chest tightness, wheezing, coughing.

Prevention: Avoid known triggers (e.g., pollen, smoke), and take controller medications.

Back Pain

Symptoms: Muscle ache, shooting pain, pain that radiates down the leg.

Prevention: Regular exercise, maintain good posture, and manage weight.

Cancer

Symptoms: Lumps, unexplained weight changes, fatigue, persistent pain, skin changes.

Prevention: Avoid tobacco, eat a healthy diet, maintain a healthy weight, get vaccinated.

COPD

Symptoms: Shortness of breath, persistent cough with mucus, wheezing, chest tightness.

Prevention: Avoid smoking, reduce exposure to air pollution and occupational dusts.

Depression

Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in sleep or appetite, fatigue.

Prevention: Build strong social connections, manage stress, and seek early help.

Diabetes

Symptoms: Increased thirst and urination, increased hunger, fatigue, blurred vision.

Prevention: Maintain a healthy weight, eat a balanced diet, and be physically active.

Epilepsy

Symptoms: Recurrent seizures, temporary confusion, staring spells, loss of consciousness.

Prevention: Prevent head injuries, ensure adequate perinatal care, manage risk factors.

Headache

Symptoms: Pressure or tightness, severe throbbing, pain around one eye.

Prevention: Manage stress, maintain regular sleep and meal schedules, avoid known triggers.

Heart Disease

Symptoms: Chest pain, shortness of breath, pain in the arms, shoulder, or jaw.

Prevention: Healthy diet, regular exercise, no smoking, and managing blood pressure.

Hypertension

Symptoms: Often none ("silent killer"), but can include headaches, dizziness, or nosebleeds.

Prevention: Reduce salt intake, eat a balanced diet, exercise, and limit alcohol.

Kidney Disease

Symptoms: Fatigue, trouble concentrating, swollen feet/ankles, frequent urination.

Prevention: Manage blood pressure and blood sugar, reduce salt intake, do not smoke.

Obesity

Symptoms: Excess body fat, shortness of breath, fatigue, joint and back pain.

Prevention: Maintain a healthy diet with portion control and engage in regular physical activity.

Stroke

Symptoms: Sudden numbness or weakness (especially on one side), confusion, trouble speaking.

Prevention: Control high blood pressure, don't smoke, maintain a healthy diet and weight.
