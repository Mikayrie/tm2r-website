Headache Disorders

Headache disorders are among the most common disorders of the nervous system. They are characterized by recurrent headache and are associated with personal and societal burdens of pain, disability, damaged quality of life and financial cost. The most common are tension-type headache (TTH), migraine, and medication-overuse headache (MOH).

Symptoms

Symptoms vary depending on the type of headache. Common types and their symptoms include:

  • Tension-type headache: Often described as a pressure or tightness, like a band around the head.
  • Migraine: A primary headache disorder causing severe throbbing or pulsing pain, often on one side of the head, accompanied by nausea and sensitivity to light and sound.
  • Cluster headache: Severe, recurring headaches that are experienced in cycles (clusters), followed by remission periods. The pain is intense and often centered around one eye.

Causes and Risk Factors

A headache can be a primary condition or a symptom of another condition. Primary headaches are not caused by another disease. The exact causes are not always clear but may involve genetics and environmental triggers (World Health Organization, 2023).

  • Triggers: Common triggers can include stress, lack of sleep, certain foods and drinks (like alcohol and caffeine), and poor posture.
  • Medication Overuse: Frequent use of pain-relief medication for headaches can itself lead to more headaches.
  • Secondary Causes: Headaches can also be caused by other problems, such as head injury, sinus infection, or, in rare cases, a brain tumour.

Prevention and Management

Appropriate treatment for headache disorders depends on the type of headache and other factors. Management can include medication, lifestyle changes, and stress management.

  • Pain Relievers: Over-the-counter pain relievers can be effective for tension headaches.
  • Prescription Medications: For migraines and cluster headaches, specific medications (like triptans) may be needed.
  • Lifestyle Adjustments: Regular sleep, meals, and exercise can help prevent some headaches. Identifying and avoiding triggers is also key.
  • Stress Management: Techniques such as biofeedback and relaxation therapy can be beneficial.
