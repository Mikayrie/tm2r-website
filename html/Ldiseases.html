Lifestyle Diseases

These diseases are often linked to a person's lifestyle choices and can be prevented through healthy habits.

Allergies

Symptoms: Sneezing, runny nose, itchy or watery eyes, skin rash, hives.

Prevention: Avoid known allergens, use air purifiers, and take prescribed medications.

Learn More

Asthma

Symptoms: Shortness of breath, chest tightness, wheezing, and coughing.

Prevention: Avoid triggers like pollen and smoke, follow a doctor's action plan.

Learn More

Cancer

Symptoms: Varies widely; may include lumps, unexplained weight loss, or fatigue.

Prevention: Healthy diet, no smoking, sun protection, regular medical screenings.

Learn More

COPD

Symptoms: Chronic cough, shortness of breath, wheezing, excess mucus.

Prevention: The most effective prevention is to not smoke and avoid secondhand smoke.

Learn More

Diabetes

Symptoms: Increased thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision, fatigue.

Prevention: Maintain healthy weight, be physically active, eat a healthy diet.

Learn More

Heart Disease

Symptoms: Chest pain, shortness of breath, pain in the neck, jaw, or back.

Prevention: Healthy diet, regular exercise, manage blood pressure and cholesterol.

Learn More

High Cholesterol

Symptoms: Usually has no symptoms and is detected through a blood test.

Prevention: Eat a low-fat, high-fiber diet, exercise regularly, maintain a healthy weight.

Learn More

Hypertension

Symptoms: Often none, but high pressure can cause headaches, blurred vision.

Prevention: Healthy diet, reduced salt, regular exercise, limiting alcohol.

Learn More

Obesity

Symptoms: Excessive body fat that increases the risk of other health problems.

Prevention: A balanced diet, regular physical activity, and consistent portion control.

Learn More

Stroke

Symptoms: Sudden numbness or weakness, especially on one side of the body.

Prevention: Control high blood pressure, manage diabetes, maintain a healthy diet.

Learn More
