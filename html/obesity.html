Obesity

Overweight and obesity are defined as abnormal or excessive fat accumulation that may impair health. Obesity is a complex chronic disease. The causes are understood to be a result of a combination of factors including genetics, environment and socioeconomic determinants. It increases the risk of other diseases like cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, and certain cancers.

Symptoms

Obesity is diagnosed when your body mass index (BMI) is 30 or higher. The main symptom is excess body fat, which increases the risk of serious health problems.

  • Excess body fat, especially around the waist.
  • Shortness of breath and increased sweating.
  • Fatigue and joint or back pain.
  • Low confidence and self-esteem.

Causes and Risk Factors

The fundamental cause of obesity is an energy imbalance between calories consumed and calories expended. Globally, there has been an increased intake of energy-dense foods that are high in fat and sugars, and a decrease in physical activity due to the increasingly sedentary nature of many forms of work and transportation (World Health Organization, 2024).

  • Diet: A diet high in calories, lacking in fruits and vegetables, full of fast food, and loaded with high-calorie beverages.
  • Inactivity: A sedentary lifestyle means you don't burn as many calories.
  • Genetics: Genes can affect the amount of body fat you store and where that fat is distributed.
  • Social and Economic Issues: Lack of access to healthy food or safe areas for exercise can contribute.

Prevention and Management

Obesity is a treatable disease. Supportive environments and communities are fundamental in shaping peopleâ€™s choices. Management involves lifestyle changes, and in some cases, medication or surgery.

  • Dietary Changes: Reduce the intake of calories from fats and sugars and increase consumption of fruits, vegetables, legumes, whole grains and nuts.
  • Physical Activity: Engage in regular physical activity (60 minutes a day for children and 150 minutes spread through the week for adults).
  • Behavioural Changes: Counselling and support groups can help address emotional and behavioural issues related to eating.
  • Medication and Surgery: For some people, prescription medications or weight-loss surgery may be options.
