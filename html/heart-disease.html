Cardiovascular Diseases (Heart Disease)

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are a group of disorders of the heart and blood vessels. They include coronary heart disease, cerebrovascular disease, rheumatic heart disease and other conditions. Heart attacks and strokes are acute events mainly caused by a blockage that prevents blood from flowing to the heart or brain.

Symptoms

There are often no symptoms of the underlying disease of the blood vessels. A heart attack or stroke may be the first sign. Symptoms of a heart attack include:

  • Pain or discomfort in the centre of the chest, and/or in the arms, left shoulder, elbows, jaw, or back.
  • Difficulty in breathing or shortness of breath.
  • Feeling sick or vomiting; feeling light-headed or faint.
  • A cold sweat and becoming pale.

Causes and Risk Factors

The most important behavioural risk factors of heart disease and stroke are unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, tobacco use and harmful use of alcohol. These in turn lead to raised blood pressure (hypertension), raised blood glucose (diabetes), and raised blood lipids (World Health Organization, 2024).

  • Behavioural Risks: Tobacco use, unhealthy diet, obesity, physical inactivity and alcohol abuse.
  • Metabolic Risks: Hypertension, diabetes, and high cholesterol.
  • Other factors: Age and family history also play a role.

Prevention and Management

Most cardiovascular diseases can be prevented by addressing behavioural risk factors. Early detection and management are key for people with CVD or who are at high cardiovascular risk.

  • Healthy Lifestyle: A balanced diet, regular physical activity, and avoiding tobacco and harmful alcohol use are crucial.
  • Medication: Medical therapy is often needed to control hypertension, high cholesterol, and diabetes.
  • Medical Procedures: For established CVD, procedures such as coronary artery bypass, balloon angioplasty, or valve repair may be necessary.
