High Cholesterol

Raised cholesterol increases the risks of heart disease and stroke. Globally, a third of ischaemic heart disease is attributable to high cholesterol. It is a major contributor to the buildup of fatty deposits in arteries (atherosclerosis), which can lead to a heart attack or stroke.

Symptoms & Diagnosis

High cholesterol is often called a "silent condition" because it typically has no symptoms. The only way to detect it is through a blood test. Many people remain unaware they have high cholesterol until they experience a serious health event, which is why regular screening is important.

Causes and Risk Factors

The most important modifiable risk factors for high cholesterol are an unhealthy diet (especially one high in saturated fats) and physical inactivity. Other risk factors include tobacco use, harmful use of alcohol, and being overweight or obese.

Prevention and Management

Lifestyle changes are the first line of defence against high cholesterol. This includes:

  • Reducing saturated fats found in foods like red meat and full-fat dairy products.
  • Eliminating trans fats, often found in margarines and store-bought cookies and crackers.
  • Eating foods rich in omega-3 fatty acids, such as salmon, walnuts and flaxseeds.
  • Increasing soluble fiber from foods like oatmeal, kidney beans, and apples.
  • Engaging in regular physical activity.
